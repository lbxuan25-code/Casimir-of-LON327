# d-wave exact-static phase-Hessian status

## Closed parts of the Ward chain

The complete commensurate audits keep the primitive Ward residual divided by `|q|` near `1e-15`. The Peierls/current vertices, direct/contact terms, left/right orientation and translation by the full external momentum are therefore not the active blocker.

The remaining longitudinal leakage is generated by the collective phase column and propagated into the effective EM kernel by the Schur complement.

## Bond metric

The nearest-neighbour projected bond metric is

```text
g_bond(q) = 0.5 * (cos(qx/2)^2 + cos(qy/2)^2).
```

At the original `N=628`, `(mx,my)=(3,2)` point it reduced the required counterterm mismatch from the current q-independent value to approximately `1.4286e-5`, but did not close the single-origin diagnostic.

## Half-q sublattice diagnosis

For a commensurate grid with step `h=2 pi/N` and `q=h(mx,my)`, translation by `q` is always an integer index permutation. The finite-q BdG bubble, however, uses `k +/- q/2`.

- if both `mx` and `my` are even, the endpoints remain on the same shifted tensor sublattice;
- if either component is odd, the endpoints lie on a complementary half-step sublattice while the q=0 counterterm is sampled on the original sublattice.

The fixed `N=628` axial family exposed an exact odd/even pattern:

| shift | bond multiplier error |
|---|---:|
| `(1,0)` | `1.428853056140e-5` |
| `(2,0)` | `2.220446049250e-16` |
| `(3,0)` | `1.428710036000e-5` |

At the half-q-compatible `(2,0)` point, the bond metric closes the phase column to machine precision:

```text
bond defect / |q| = 1.387075236083e-15
```

The nearly q-independent odd-shift error and the machine-precision even-shift result identify a grid-origin alias rather than a physical q-power law. The previous three-point scaling fits that mixed odd and even endpoint sublattices must not be interpreted as a phase-Hessian exponent.

## Complementary-subgrid closure at the original physical q

The original `(mx,my)=(3,2)` point was repeated on the complementary x-origin and the primitive phase-column components were averaged before forming any ratio.  The result is

Therefore the single-origin `1.4286e-5` remainder is entirely a half-q sublattice alias.  No additional physical phase-Hessian term is resolved by the commensurate audit.

```text

```

```text

```

Pulling an isotropic x/y bond HS metric back along this tangent gives


This is an exact nearest-neighbour geometry identity, not a fitted small-q correction.  The Ward derivation fixes the phase counterterm only; it does not by itself justify changing the amplitude diagonal.
